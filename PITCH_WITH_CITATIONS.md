## Separating Verified Data from Assumptions

## CRITICAL: DATA CLASSIFICATION

This pitch uses three types of claims:

1. **✅ VERIFIED** - From published, credible sources
2. **⚠️ ASSUMPTION** - Market research/educated estimates (need citation confirmation)
3. **📊 INTERNAL** - Your own MVP data (you need to prove these)

## INTERNAL DATA YOU NEED TO GATHER

### Your MVP Metrics (Most Important for Investors)

Instead of assuming market data, SHOW YOUR OWN DATA:

**✅ What you should measure:**
1. **Beta user feedback:**
   - How many women tested the app?
   - What % would book a trip?
   - What % cited safety as key factor?
   - What % interested in repeat bookings?

2. **Pre-launch signals:**
   - Waitlist signups (if you have one)
   - Email inquiries
   - Facebook group activity
   - Influencer interest

3. **Conversion metrics:**
   - How many visited your landing page?
   - How many started booking?
   - How many completed booking?
   - CAC if you ran any paid ads?

**Example:** "In our beta test with 200 women, 72% expressed interest in booking a trip if they could verify group safety" is MUCH more credible than general market claims.

## REBUILT PITCH - CONSERVATIVE VERSION

### What's Actually Defensible

**DO SAY:**

> "Women travel market in India is large and growing post-COVID. Our hypothesis is that women want community and safety when traveling together.

> We've tested this with [X] beta users. [Y]% expressed interest in booking. [Z]% cited safety as their #1 concern.

> Unit economics work: per-trip commission model with [your verified metrics].

> Zero competitors in women-focused travel space. We're building the first platform.

> Raising ₹15 Cr to prove product-market fit over 18 months."

**DON'T SAY:**

> "Women spend ₹8,500 Cr annually" (unverified)
> "67% YoY growth in solo travel" (unverified)
> "68% would pay premium" (assumed, not tested)
